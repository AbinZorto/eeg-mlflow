In this sweep, low-montage resting-state EEG supported strong patient-level discrimination of tDCS response in adults with treatment-resistant depression, and the strongest result came from an extremely sparse feature-selection regime. The best hybrid run achieved ROC-AUC 0.816 while selecting only 1 feature per fold and 7 unique recurrent features overall. Hard-set overlap across folds remained modest, but the recurrent biomarkers retained consistent effect direction and concentrated on a coherent temporal and frontal-temporal signal profile.

The main contribution of the study is to show that sparse biomarker discovery and strong participant-level discrimination can coexist in a clinically practical low-montage EEG setting. Rather than requiring a broad high-dimensional feature pool, the strongest model isolated a compact recurrent biomarker signature under strict foldwise selection and evaluation. That result strengthens the case for low-montage resting-state EEG as a viable basis for pretreatment stratification of tDCS response in treatment-resistant depression. External validation in larger cohorts is the next step.
